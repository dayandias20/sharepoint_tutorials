Platform Migration Experience: ZohoOne to Salesforce to SharePoint

"During my time at Premier Aesthetix, a solar panel sales and installation company, I led a critical CRM migration from ZohoOne to Salesforce that directly demonstrates my ability to analyze, optimize, and transform complex business processes—skills essential for HESAA's SharePoint and workflow modernization needs."

Phase 1: Understanding Existing ZohoOne Processes

Before transitioning to Salesforce, I spent the first month deeply analyzing how our solar sales team used ZohoOne:

  • Mapped 15+ existing workflows across the customer lifecycle: lead capture, site assessment scheduling, proposal generation, financing application, permit tracking, installation coordination, and post-installation support
  • Identified inefficiencies: Manual data entry between systems (ZohoOne CRM → separate financing calculator → permit tracking spreadsheets), 40% of sales team time spent on administrative tasks
  • Documented pain points: No automated follow-up for customers stuck in permit approval, inconsistent proposal templates causing 25% rejection rate, lack of integration with utility rebate databases

Key Insight

The processes weren't broken—they were just fragmented across multiple tools. The goal wasn't to replicate ZohoOne in Salesforce, but to consolidate and streamline.

Phase 2: Designing Enhanced Salesforce Processes

I architected Salesforce solutions that mirrored ZohoOne's functionality while introducing automation and integration:

Example 1: Automated Customer Lifecycle Tracking (Relevant to HESAA's Student Lifecycle)

ZohoOne Process:

  • Sales reps manually updated lead status across 8 stages (Inquiry → Site Visit → Proposal → Financing → Permitting → Installation → Activation → Service)
  • Financing team separately tracked loan applications in spreadsheets
  • Permit coordinators used email to track municipal approvals

Salesforce Solution:

  • Custom objects and record types: Created "Solar Opportunity" object with automated stage progression based on trigger events
  • Salesforce Flow automation: When customer accepts proposal → automatically creates financing record → triggers workflow to document team for permit package preparation
  • Integration with third-party systems: Connected to LoanPal API (solar financing platform) to auto-populate credit decisions, similar to how HESAA might integrate with FAFSA or loan servicers
  • Automated notifications: When permit approved by municipality → triggers installation scheduling flow → sends automated email to customer with timeline

Result: Reduced customer onboarding time from 45 days to 28 days (38% improvement), decreased manual status updates by 60%

HESAA Parallel: Just as I automated the solar customer journey from inquiry to installation, I can design SharePoint/Power Automate workflows for student aid applications—from FAFSA submission → document verification → eligibility determination → award notification → disbursement tracking. The multi-stage, compliance-heavy process is nearly identical.

Example 2: Document Management & Approval Workflows (Directly Relevant to HESAA)

ZohoOne Process:

  • Solar installation contracts, HOA approval letters, utility interconnection agreements, and permit documents stored in ZohoOne's basic document library
  • Approval routing done via email: Sales manager → Legal → Finance → Customer signature
  • No version control; 15% of contracts had to be re-executed due to outdated terms or missing signatures

Salesforce Solution:

  • Salesforce Files + Custom Approval Process: Built document library with metadata tagging (document type, customer ID, permit jurisdiction, expiration date)
  • Multi-level approval workflow: When sales rep uploads contract → triggers Salesforce approval process → routes to sales manager (verifies pricing) → legal (verifies compliance with state solar regulations) → finance (verifies payment terms) → auto-generates DocuSign envelope for customer
  • Version control and audit trail: All document revisions tracked with timestamps and user attribution
  • Automated expiration alerts: Permits expire after 6-12 months—built Flow to send alerts 30 days before expiration with renewal instructions

Result: Contract error rate dropped from 15% to 3%, approval cycle time reduced from 7 days to 2 days, 100% audit trail for regulatory compliance

HESAA Parallel: This experience directly translates to HESAA's need for managing student financial aid documents (verification forms, tax documents, scholarship applications). I can implement SharePoint document libraries with Power Automate approval workflows that route documents through financial aid counselors → compliance officers → disbursement team, with full audit trails for federal/state reporting requirements.
